Design Process



Competitive Analysis

Assess the strengths and weaknesses of competitors' products relative to yours, in order to make recommendations for potential improvements on your product. This one was done for theoretical client Alfa Romeo Automobiles, comparatively assessing their website next to those of the Audi, Porsche, and Lotus companies.

Task Analysis

Identify a user's thought process and desired reactions toward a given task, to gain insight into how a user accomplishes said task. This one was done for theoretical client Tufts Technology Services, focusing on how a computer repair technician goes about determining if an Apple Macbook's technical issues (not being able to turn on are from a faulty motherboard, and then deciding how to proceed with the client on the matter.

Personas

Create fictional characters that represent different user types that might use your product, to help to guide decisions on designing it by considering potential users' goals, desires, and limitations. This one was done for theoretical client The Salomon Group, a sports company who wanted to make an app for skiers that incorporated functionalities determined to be of high value to the target demographic.

Storyboards

Visually depict scenarios with storyboards. A storyboard helps cast personas in stories, showing the benefits of proposed solutions to a problem. I did this one for my currently ongoing college senior year design project where my team and I, in conjuction with our sponsors from Draper, are investigating modality alerts in semi-autonomous cars.

Wireframes

Visually communicate design concepts with users, team members, and clients to generate feedback. I generated these wireframes for a web programming course group project, where my team and I were designing a prototype for a web gaming application. These wireframes in particular were early on in the team's brainstorming sessions; I drew these up to help prompt discussion about possible designs.

User Flows

User flows help with understanding how a proposed design interacts with user behavior, helping visualize the details and specifics of different interactions. Insights from this help inform the creation and reevaluation of mockups/prototypes. I created this user flow diagram for theoretical client Me-2-You, a two-way clothing donation platform that allowed donors and recipients to interact with each other. This allowed my team and I to consider potential user interactions with our proposed website designs, and refine them from there.

Mockups/Prototypes

Mockups and prototypes enable designers to uncover usability issues and validate design decisions based on actual user reactions. They allow for easy distribution amongst test users to observe how well a design will be received before building the full-scale product. I constructed this high fidelity mockup at a hackathon at Tufts University, the Women in Computer Science (WiCS) group at Tufts; they were looking for a website design that could help promote their mission to empower women in the field of computer science.
